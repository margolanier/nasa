Missions

Past

Kennedy Space Center was created in 1958 during the administration of President Eisenhower for the Apollo manned lunar landing program. It has evolved to meet the changing needs of America's manned space program.

Apollo Program, 1970's

The Apollo Program included both manned and unmanned space missions, flown by NASA between 1961 and 1975. They culminated with a series of manned Moon landings between 1969 and 1972.
  • Unmanned missions: the main task of these missions was to test the various Apollo components like launch vehicles and spacecraft.
  • Manned Missions: A total of fifteen Saturn V vehicles were ordered which would have been enough for three more Moon landing missions through Apollo 20. This flight was cancelled during the first Apollo 11 landing mission, to make the launch vehicle available for the Skylab space station. Shortly thereafter, Apollo 18 and 19 were cancelled in response to Congressional cuts in NASA's budget.

Space Shuttle of 1980's-2000's

Shuttle missions during nearly 30 years of operations included:
  • deploying satellites and interplanetary probes
  • conducting space science and technology experiments
  • visits to the Russian MIR space station
  • construction and servicing of the International Space Station
  • deployment and servicing of the Hubble Space Telescope
  • serving as a space laboratory
The shuttle was retired from service in July 2011 after 135 launches.

Present, 2010's

NASA is currently designing the next heavy launch vehicle known as the Space Launch System (SLS) for continuation of human spaceflight.
