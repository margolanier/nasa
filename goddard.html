Missions

Past

Goddard's primary work has been involved in designing, building, and operating spacecraft. The list of these missions reflects a diverse set of scientific objectives and goals.

  • Explorer 1: Nation's first artificial satellite
  • Landsat: Series of spacecraft that studied the Earth's resources
  • TIROS-1: Weather satellites
  • Spartan platform: Low-cost 2-3 day missions deployed from the space shuttle
  • Compton Gamma Ray Observatory: Second of NASA's Great Observatories
  • Cosmic Background Explorer: Space science observatory that provided data about the early universe

Present

Goddard currently supports the operation of dozens of spacecraft collecting scientific data.

  • Earth Observing System (EOS): Includes the Terra, Aqua, and Aura spacecraft flying alongside several projects from other Centers
  • Tropical Rainfall Measurement Mission (TRMM): provides data critical to hurricane predictions
  • Hubble Space Telescope: carried into orbit by a Space Shuttle in 1990 and remains in operation. In low Earth orbit, Hubble's four main instruments observe in the near ultraviolet, visible, and near infrared spectra.
  • Wilkinson Microwave Anisotropy Probe (WMAP): Studies the structure and evolution of the universe
  • Solar and Heliospheric Observatory (SOHO): Studying the Sun and how its behavior affects life on the Earth
